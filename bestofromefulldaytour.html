Tour Description

Visit the must-see sites of the Eternal City with a local expert

This is the perfect tour for those who have only one day in Rome and wish to see the highlights of the Eternal City.

Our tour begins at the largest amphitheatre ever built, theColosseum. There is no queuing as you enterwith pre-booked ticketsand enjoy a guided tour of the magnificent interior and seating levels. Outside once again you will see theArch of Constantine, built in 315 A.D. to celebrate Constantine’s victory over Maxentius three years earlier.

We then take a walk through the central piazza of Ancient Rome, the Forum where we see the Arch of Titus, the House of the Vestal Virgins, the Temple of Saturn, the Senate House and the Arch of Septimus Severus. You follow the Sacred Way to reach Capitol Hill where you will see the magnificentVittoriano, modern Italy’s greatest marble monument, built to celebrate Italy’s unification by the King Victor Emanuel II.

A short walk brings us to the ruins of an Ancient Roman Aqueduct followed by the splendour of theTrevi Fountain. Created by Niccolò Salvi in 1735 this is perhaps the most most well-known and beautiful fountain in the world. It is a popular custom to throw a coin in the fountain to ensure that you will return to Rome one day!

We stop here for a lunch break and your guide will suggest a number of excellent places to get a sandwich, a pizza or a three-course meal. After lunch the tour continues to the Government District to see theParliamentand thePalace of the Council of Ministers, two magnificent buildings of the Italian Republic. In front of the palace stands a masterpiece of II century Roman art, the marble Column ofMarcus Aurelius.

Another short walk brings us to the Temple of Hadrian, built in his honour by his successor the Emperor Antoninus Pius. Behind the original marble columns is now home to thelocal Chamber of Commerce. Our next stop, the Pantheon, is reached via a narrow, charming street. This is the best-preserved temple of Ancient Rome and houses the Tomb of the painter Raphael.

We then cross theAncient Baths of Neroand arrive in Piazza Navona, theAncient Stadium built by Emperor Domitian. This beautiful, elegant square was decorated by Bernini and other XVI century masters. Here amongst the many churches and fountains you will see the famous Fountain of the Four Rivers.

Via a bridge across the River Tiber you will arrive to the highlight of the tour, a visit to the Vatican City. Here you can enjoy an exclusive tour of the Vatican Museums andSt Peter’s Basilica. No waiting in line on this VIP tour! Your guide takes you to see the Sistine Chapel with Michelangelo’s famous ceiling followed by a visit to the Pope’s apartments with its frescoes by Raphael.

Is it possible to see Rome in a day? Yes! This private guided tour is a great way to visit all the “must-see” sights of Rome withno queues and no stress!
